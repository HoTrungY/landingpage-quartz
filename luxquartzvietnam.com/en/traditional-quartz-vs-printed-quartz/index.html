Traditional Quartz vs Printed Quartz: Exploring the Present and Future

23/01/2025     Lux Quartz 1

Quartz surfaces have increasingly become popular in interior design and construction because of their durability, beauty, and adaptability. As the development of the industry, the appearance of printed quartz surfaces is creating interest and innovation. In this blog, let’s explore the difference between traditional quartz and printed quartz, what their benefits are, and get an overview of both options.

What Are Traditional Quartz Slabs?

Traditional quartz slabs are engineered surfaces crafted from 90-93% of natural quartz grits combined with resins and pigments. This mixture creates a non-porous and durable surface that mimics the beauty of natural stone while providing excellent performance. A defining feature of traditional quartz slabs is veins that run throughout the thickness of the slab, ensuring a consistent and natural look even after cutting or shaping.  It’s a popular choice for kitchens, bathrooms, and commercial areas due to its practicality and aesthetic appeal.

Key Features of Traditional Quartz Slabs:

Durability: Highly resistant to scratches, stains, and impacts, perfect for busy spaces.

Design Flexibility: Available in various colors and patterns, including designs inspired by marble and granite.

Through-Body Veining: Provides a uniform and natural look, maintaining its beauty even after being cut and shaped

Low Maintenance: With non-porous surfaces, it needs just a little cleaning and is resistant to bacteria and moisture.

What Are Printed Quartz Surfaces?

Printed quartz surfaces are similar to traditional quartz slabs but use digital printing technology to print complex designs directly onto the slab’s surface. This modern technique allows highly detailed and customizable patterns, making it possible to achieve unique looks that traditional methods cannot make. The demand for printed quartz is growing in luxury and custom projects, attracting clients who desire unique visual styles.

Key Features of Printed Quartz Surfaces:

Customization: Provides endless design options, featuring unique and creative patterns.

Modern Appeal: Perfect for modern spaces that focus on individual style.

Surface-Level Design: The design is applied to the surface only, which may result in less consistent vein patterns on the edges compared to traditional quartz, where the veins run throughout the slab’s thickness.

Comparing Traditional Quartz and Printed Quartz

When deciding between traditional quartz and printed quartz, it really comes down to what your project needs most. If you value timeless durability, a natural look, and versatility for various settings, traditional quartz is an exceptional choice. Its through-body veining provides consistent beauty, even after making custom cuts, and its non-porous surface means it’s easy to maintain.

On the other hand, printed quartz is perfect for those looking for innovative customization and distinctive designs, especially in luxury or highly personalized projects. However, it may come with higher costs and require more maintenance.

For most projects, traditional quartz offers a proven balance of style, durability, and practicality, making it the go-to choice for both residential and commercial applications.
